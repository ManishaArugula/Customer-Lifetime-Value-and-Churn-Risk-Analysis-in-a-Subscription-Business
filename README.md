# Revenue & Churn Risk Analysis in a Subscription Business

## 1. Project Overview

This project analyses Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) and churn risk in a B2B SaaS subscription business to understand how customer value is distributed across segments and how churn within those segments impacts overall revenue. The objective is to support data-driven customer prioritisation and targeted retention strategies focused on high-impact segments.

## 2. Business Problem

The business is experiencing customer churn that directly impacts recurring revenue. While growth remains strong, it is unclear which customer segments contribute most to overall revenue, which segments contribute least, and where churn poses the greatest revenue risk. The business requires a clear, segment-level view of value concentration and churn impact to prioritise retention efforts effectively.

RQ: Which customer segments contributed the most and least to observed customer lifetime value during 2023–2024?

## 3. Goals

1. Understand how customer value is distributed across customer segments and acquisition cohorts

2. Identify which segments drive the most and least observed value

3. Quantify the revenue impact of churn across segments

4. Support targeted, high-impact retention strategies rather than uniform churn reduction

## 4. Key Metrics

#### Primary KPI

-> Customer Lifetime Value (Observed CLV)
For this project the total MRR generated by each customer over the span of 2 years is considered as the CLV

#### Secondary KPIs

-> Customer Churn Rate (by segment and cohort)

-> Revenue Contribution by Segment (share of total CLV)

#### Supporting KPIs

-> Average Revenue

-> Average Revenue per Customer

## 7. Business Rules & Definitions
1.Customer Churn

-> Defined at the account level using churn_flag from the accounts table

->Subscription-level churn flags and churn events are not used to infer churn timing due to inconsistent alignment with revenue cessation

->Churn events table is only used for supporting information and not to define churn

2.Acquisition Cohorts

->Defined by the month of the first paid subscription start date

->Account-level, month-based (YYYY-MM)

->Used to compare CLV and churn patterns across 2023–2024 cohorts

3.Revenue & CLV

->Revenue sourced from mrr_amount in the subscriptions table

->Revenue aggregated to the account level to avoid double counting

->CLV represents observed cumulative revenue during 2023–2024, not a predictive lifetime estimate

## 8. SQL Analysis 

SQL analysis was conducted to analyze the data and assess the value of customers, churn, and operations within the CRM data set. The analysis aimed to determine the revenue and churn for acquisition cohorts, value segments, and subscription plan tiers. It also aimed to analyze the relationship between customer support escalations and revenue risks. The goal was to determine which type of customers were generating the most revenue, which type of customers were causing the most churn and thus creating the highest revenue risks, and which operations were impacting customers.

### 8.1 Key-Findings:

1. Acquisition cohort performance
Customers acquired in mid-to-late 2024 have the highest observed level of CLV but also have the highest level of churned revenue, suggesting that recent cohorts with high growth are providing much of the revenue but also have the highest level of revenue volatility.

2. Revenue risk by customer value segment
Customers with low value have the highest level of churn but also have the lowest level of churned revenue, suggesting that retaining high-value customers is much more important to retaining revenue.

3. Support escalations and churn risk
Customers with support escalations have slightly higher churned revenue contribution compared to those without support escalations, suggesting that support escalations may not be the primary cause of churn.

4. Cohorts contributing most to churned revenue
Customers acquired in mid-to-late 2024 have the highest level of churned revenue, with the September 2024 cohort having the highest level of churned revenue, supporting the volatility observed in the most recent cohorts.

5. Plan tier revenue vs operational effort
Enterprise plans have the highest level of CLV but also have the lowest level of support effort per level of revenue, while Basic plans have the lowest level of revenue but also have the highest level of support effort.

## 10. Customer Segmentation Summary
Customer Segmentation (K-Means) - Key Findings
K-Means clustering was performed with the following metrics: customer engagement, support activity, revenue, and churn rate to understand the behavior of customers and the effect of engagement and support on the churn rate.

**Cluster 0 - Low Value and Low Engagement (Churn Rate: 20.8%)**
This cluster has the least amount of revenue generated by the customers (average MRR ~19.6) with the least usage of the product features (14 features) and the least support interactions.

**Cluster 1 - High Value and High Engagement (Churn Rate: 23.5%)**
This cluster has the maximum amount of revenue generated by the customers (average MRR ~30.7) with the maximum product usage (~601 times) and zero support escalations.

**Cluster 2 - Engaged and Friction Prone (Highest Risk) (Churn Rate: 24.7%)**
This cluster has the maximum engagement (~561 times) and the maximum revenue generated by the customers (average MRR ~30.0) but has the highest churn rate and the maximum escalations (81).

**Cluster 3 - Support Dependent and Loyal Customers (Lowest Churn Rate: 19.0%)**
This cluster has moderate revenue generated by the customers (average MRR ~28.5) with the maximum support interactions (726 tickets) and the least churn rate.

## 11. Strategic Interpretation

Customer churn cannot be explained by value or engagement alone; while low-value customers churn due to weak product adoption, the highest churn risk (24.7%) occurs among highly engaged customers experiencing operational friction. The analysis reveals two engaged segments: one relatively stable with moderate support dependency and another where escalations and unresolved issues significantly increase churn risk.

## 12. Retention Strategy by Segment

Cluster 0 – Low Value, Low Engagement: Enhance product adoption through nudges and automated campaigns.

Cluster 1 – High Value, High Engagement: Safeguard high-value revenue streams through retention efforts such as personalized communication and loyalty programs.

Cluster 2 – Engaged and Friction-Prone (Highest Priority): Mitigate churn risks by focusing on issue resolution, enhancing product quality, and increasing support responsiveness.

Cluster 3 – Support Dependent and Loyal: Maintain high retention through support and service-level agreements.
